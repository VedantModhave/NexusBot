Fee Payment and Refund Policy

The fee structure for all programmes at XYZ College is approved by the State Fee Regulatory Committee and is revised annually before the start of each academic year. Any changes in fees are communicated to students at least 60 days before the new session through the college website and student portal.

The tuition fee for the B.Tech programme is Rs 1,25,000 per semester, which includes tuition charges, laboratory fees, and library access fees. For the MBA programme, the tuition fee is Rs 2,00,000 per semester, covering tuition, case study materials, and industry visit charges.

Fee payment can be made through multiple modes including online payment via the student portal using UPI, net banking, or credit and debit cards. Students may also submit demand drafts payable to XYZ College of Engineering and Technology drawn on any nationalised bank, or make a direct bank transfer.

All fees must be paid within 30 days of the semester commencement date. Late payment attracts a penalty of Rs 100 per day for the first 15 days and Rs 200 per day thereafter. Students who fail to pay fees within 60 days of the due date will have their semester registration cancelled.

Students experiencing genuine financial hardship may approach the Accounts Office to request an instalment plan. A maximum of three equal instalments spread across the first three months is permitted. An instalment request form along with a letter from the parent or guardian must be submitted.

Additional charges include a laboratory fee of Rs 15,000 per semester for science and engineering students, an activity fee of Rs 5,000 per year for cultural and sports events, an examination fee of Rs 3,000 per semester, and a student welfare fund contribution of Rs 2,000 per year.

Management quota admissions carry a higher fee of Rs 2,50,000 per semester for B.Tech and Rs 3,50,000 for MBA. Students admitted under management quota must still meet the minimum eligibility criteria for their programme.

The refund policy for course withdrawal allows 90 percent refund within 15 days of admission, 75 percent within 30 days, 50 percent within 60 days, and no refund after 60 days. A caution deposit of Rs 10,000 is collected at admission and is fully refundable upon completion of the programme.

The college has tie-ups with State Bank of India, Bank of Baroda, and HDFC Bank for education loans. Students with family income below Rs 4,50,000 per annum are eligible for interest subsidy under government loan schemes.

Fee receipts are generated automatically for online payments and can be downloaded from the student portal. For offline payments, receipts are issued from the Accounts Office within 3 working days.


Scholarship and Financial Aid

XYZ College operates a comprehensive scholarship programme administered through the Scholarship Cell under the Dean of Student Affairs. Both merit-based and need-based scholarships are available to support students from all backgrounds.

The Principal Gold Medal Scholarship provides 100 percent tuition fee waiver for students maintaining a CGPA of 9.5 or above. This is the most prestigious award at the institution, given to a maximum of 10 students college-wide each year.

The Dean List Scholarship provides 50 percent tuition fee waiver for students with a CGPA of 9.0 or above. Approximately 30 students receive this scholarship each year, and it is renewable each semester as long as the student maintains the required CGPA.

The Departmental Merit Award gives Rs 25,000 per year to the top three students in each department based on academic performance. This recognises consistent excellence at the department level and is presented during the annual convocation.

Students from economically weaker sections with family income below Rs 8,00,000 per annum qualify for the Government Post-Matric Scholarship through the National Scholarship Portal. The college also offers its own Access for All scholarship covering 75 percent of tuition for families with income below Rs 3,00,000.

SC and ST students are eligible for the Central Government Post-Matric Scholarship covering full tuition plus Rs 3,000 monthly maintenance for hostellers and Rs 1,500 for day scholars. OBC students with income below Rs 2,50,000 qualify for a separate scholarship.

Sports scholarships of up to Rs 50,000 per year plus 50 percent fee concession are available for students who have represented their state or nation in recognised sports. The Vidya Lakshmi scholarship gives Rs 30,000 annually to girl students with CGPA above 8.0.

Research Innovation Grants of up to Rs 1,00,000 support students undertaking research projects under faculty guidance. Final year students may also apply for AICTE research fellowships through the R and D Cell.

All scholarship applications are accepted online through the student portal from July 1 to August 15 each year. Required documents include income certificates, mark sheets, caste certificates where applicable, and a recommendation letter from the HOD. Results are announced by September 15.

Scholarship amounts are credited directly to the student bank account linked to their Aadhaar number. Students must ensure their bank details are updated on the student portal before the application deadline.


Examination and Assessment Policy

The examination system follows the Choice Based Credit System as mandated by the University Grants Commission. All examinations are conducted under the supervision of the Controller of Examinations with strict adherence to academic integrity.

End-semester examinations take place in May for the even semester and December for the odd semester. The date sheet is published on the college website and student portal at least 30 days before examinations begin. Students must have minimum 75 percent attendance to be eligible.

The evaluation comprises 30 percent internal assessment and 70 percent end-semester examination. Internal assessment includes two mid-term exams of 20 marks each where the better score counts, three quizzes of 10 marks each where the best two are counted, assignments, and class participation marks.

Mid-term examinations are held in the 6th and 12th week of each semester. Each mid-term is one hour long and covers the syllabus taught up to that point. Timetables for mid-terms are displayed on department notice boards one week in advance.

Students must score a minimum of 40 percent in each subject to pass. Both internal assessment and end-semester components must be passed separately. A student who fails internal assessment cannot pass the subject regardless of end-semester performance.

The college uses a 10-point CGPA grading system. The grades are O for Outstanding at 10 points, A Plus at 9, A at 8, B Plus at 7, B at 6, C at 5, P for Pass at 4, and F for Fail at 0. The CGPA is the weighted average across all completed semesters.

Students failing in up to three subjects may apply for supplementary examinations held in July for even semester and February for odd semester. The supplementary fee is Rs 500 per subject, and applications must be submitted within 15 days of result declaration.

Results are published within 30 days of the last exam on the college website and student portal. SMS notifications are sent to registered mobile numbers. Mark sheets are distributed within 60 days by the examination cell.

Any examination malpractice is dealt with severely. Use of unauthorized materials results in an F grade and one-semester suspension. Impersonation leads to expulsion of both students involved. Communication during exams results in an F grade for that subject.

Students may apply for revaluation of answer sheets within 10 days of result declaration. The revaluation fee is Rs 500 per subject, refundable if the grade changes. Three faculty members independently review revaluation requests.


Attendance and Leave Policy

Regular attendance is mandatory for all enrolled students at XYZ College. Biometric attendance systems installed in all classrooms and laboratories track attendance in real time, with records accessible through the student portal.

A minimum of 75 percent attendance in each individual course is required for end-semester examination eligibility. Attendance is calculated separately for theory classes, laboratory sessions, and tutorials.

Students who fall ill must obtain a medical certificate from the college health centre or a recognised government hospital. Self-certification is accepted for absences up to three consecutive days. Longer absences require a medical certificate with doctor registration number.

Students representing the college in sports, cultural events, or academic conferences may apply for duty leave through their HOD. Duty leave counts as present for attendance purposes and must be approved by both the HOD and Dean of Student Affairs before the event.

Students with attendance between 65 and 75 percent may apply for condonation by paying Rs 1,000 per subject. Attendance below 65 percent results in automatic detention, requiring the student to repeat the course. Detention in more than two subjects means repeating the entire semester.

Attendance records are shared monthly with parents through the parent portal. Parents can log in using credentials provided at admission to view attendance, academic performance, and fee status for their ward.

Students maintaining 90 percent or above attendance throughout the year receive a Certificate of Regularity during the annual day celebration. This certificate carries additional weightage during campus placement interviews with partner companies.


Hostel Accommodation and Conduct

Hostel accommodation is available for both male and female students on the XYZ College campus. The boys hostel has 400 rooms and the girls hostel has 200 rooms, all managed by the hostel administration under the Chief Warden.

Hostel admission is open to students whose permanent residence is more than 30 kilometres from campus. Applications are accepted through the student portal during admissions. Room allotment follows a first-come-first-served basis with priority for students from distant locations.

First-year students are assigned shared rooms with two per room. Second year and above may apply for single rooms based on availability. Each room is furnished with beds, study tables, chairs, cupboards, and bookshelves.

The hostel fee is Rs 60,000 per semester for shared rooms and Rs 90,000 for single rooms. This covers accommodation, three daily meals, Wi-Fi at 50 Mbps, laundry, and access to all common facilities.

The hostel mess operates cooperatively through elected student committees. Monthly mess charges are approximately Rs 4,500, adjusted quarterly based on expenditure. Vegetarian, Jain, and medically prescribed diets are accommodated on request.

Hostel gate timings are 9 PM on weekdays and 10 PM on weekends. Late entry after these times requires written permission from the warden. Frequent gate timing violations may result in disciplinary action including temporary suspension from the hostel.

Mess timings are breakfast from 7:30 to 9:00 AM, lunch from 12:30 to 2:00 PM, evening snacks from 5:00 to 6:00 PM, and dinner from 7:30 to 9:00 PM. Students must carry their mess card for entry. Weekly menus are decided by the mess committee and displayed every Saturday.

Common facilities include a TV room, indoor games with table tennis and carrom, a gymnasium, a reading room open until midnight, 24-hour water and electricity with inverter backup, and free Wi-Fi throughout the premises. A sick room with a duty nurse is available around the clock.

The girls hostel has 24/7 security with female wardens, CCTV surveillance, and biometric entry. It has its own dedicated mess and gym. Visitors are restricted to the common area between 4 and 6 PM on weekdays.

Alcohol, tobacco, and controlled substances are strictly prohibited on hostel premises. Violation results in immediate expulsion from the hostel. Ragging in any form is a criminal offence leading to expulsion from both the hostel and the institution.


Library and Digital Resources

The Central Library of XYZ College houses over 50,000 volumes of textbooks, reference books, and journals across all disciplines. It serves as the primary academic resource centre for students, faculty, and research scholars.

The library is open from 8 AM to 10 PM on weekdays and from 9 AM to 6 PM on weekends and public holidays. During examination periods starting two weeks before end-semester exams, the library extends its hours and stays open until midnight.

Undergraduate students can borrow up to 4 books for 14 days, postgraduate students can borrow 6 books for 21 days, and faculty can borrow 10 books for an entire semester. Books may be renewed once for an additional period equal to the original borrowing period.

Late return attracts a fine of Rs 5 per book per day. Lost or damaged books require the borrower to pay the replacement cost plus a processing fee of Rs 200. Reference materials and periodicals must be used within the library premises.

The digital library provides access to IEEE Xplore, Springer, Elsevier ScienceDirect, ACM Digital Library, and NPTEL video lectures. Students access these resources with their student ID at library.xyzcollege.edu.in. Off-campus access is available through the college VPN.

A self-service printing and photocopying centre is available in the library. Black and white prints cost Rs 2 per page, colour prints cost Rs 10 per page, and scanning costs Rs 5 per page. Students receive Rs 200 prepaid printing credit per semester.

Group study rooms on the first floor can be booked through the library portal for up to 3 hours. Library cards are issued automatically upon admission and activated at the circulation desk within the first week. There is no separate membership fee.

Students must maintain silence at all times in the library. Mobile phones must be on silent mode and calls taken outside. Food and beverages are not permitted in reading areas. The library also houses a rare books collection accessible by appointment.


Placement and Career Services

The Training and Placement Cell at XYZ College is dedicated to preparing students for successful careers through comprehensive training, industry engagement, and campus recruitment drives. The cell is headed by Prof. Anand Sharma and operates from Block C, Room C-101.

In 2024 the college achieved a placement rate of 92 percent with over 450 students placed across more than 80 companies. The average package was Rs 6.2 LPA, the median package was Rs 5.5 LPA, and the highest domestic package was Rs 32 LPA.

Top recruiters include TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant, Capgemini, Accenture, Amazon, Microsoft, Deloitte, L and T, and several other leading IT and core engineering companies. Over 120 companies participate in the annual placement season.

The placement cell conducts comprehensive training starting from the 5th semester. Training modules cover aptitude and reasoning, group discussion skills, mock interviews with industry professionals, resume writing, and technical skill enhancement. Sessions are held every Saturday.

Students must register on the placement portal at placements.xyzcollege.edu.in before September 1 of their final year. Eligibility requires a minimum CGPA of 6.0 with no active backlogs, and an updated resume must be uploaded at registration.

A mandatory summer internship of 6 to 8 weeks after the 6th semester gives students practical industry exposure. The placement cell helps students find internships with partner companies. Students receiving Pre-Placement Offers from their internship company may accept them before the placement season.

Students must follow the formal dress code during all placement activities. Males must wear formal trousers with full-sleeve shirts and leather shoes. Females may wear formal salwar kameez, sarees, or western formals. Casual attire is strictly prohibited during interviews.

The placement cell can be contacted at placement@xyzcollege.edu.in or by phone at +91 9876 543220 during office hours from Monday to Friday, 10 AM to 5 PM. Alumni mentorship sessions are also arranged through the placement cell for career guidance.


Transport and Commute Facilities

XYZ College provides a comprehensive bus transport service covering major areas of the city and surrounding regions. The transport department operates 12 dedicated bus routes for the convenience of day scholars commuting to campus.

Bus routes cover Shivajinagar, Kothrud, Hadapsar, Hinjewadi, Pimpri-Chinchwad, Wakad, and other key localities. Buses depart from designated pickup points at 7:30 AM and the return journey from college starts at 5:00 PM. The complete route map is available on the college website.

The transport fee is distance-based. Routes within 15 kilometres cost Rs 15,000 per semester, routes between 15 and 30 kilometres cost Rs 20,000, and routes beyond 30 kilometres cost Rs 25,000 per semester. The transport fee is payable along with tuition at the start of each semester.

Morning pickup routes begin at 6:45 AM from the farthest points, with most buses reaching campus by 8:45 AM. Exact stop timings are communicated through SMS and WhatsApp groups at the beginning of each semester. Students must be at their designated stop 5 minutes before the scheduled pickup.

Route or stop change requests can be submitted through the transport office or by emailing transport@xyzcollege.edu.in. Changes are considered at the start of each semester based on demand and bus capacity. A request form is available on the student portal.

All buses are GPS-tracked and parents can monitor the bus location through the college mobile app. Each bus has a designated conductor and driver. Emergency contact numbers for each route are shared with students and parents at the start of the academic year.

Students using private vehicles must register them with the security office and obtain a campus parking permit. Two-wheelers require a parking permit fee of Rs 1,000 per year, and four-wheelers require Rs 2,500 per year. Helmets are mandatory for two-wheeler riders on campus.


Student Grievance and Redressal

XYZ College is committed to providing a fair, transparent, and timely mechanism for addressing student grievances. The Student Grievance Redressal Committee (SGRC) handles both academic and non-academic complaints from students.

Students may file grievances through the online portal at portal.xyzcollege.edu.in under the Grievance section, through a written application to the Dean of Students, or by visiting the SGRC office in Admin Block Room A-105 from 10 AM to 4 PM on working days.

The SGRC meets fortnightly to review pending cases and ensures all complaints are resolved within 15 working days. The committee includes the Dean of Students as chairperson, two senior faculty members, the academic coordinator, and two elected student representatives.

Anonymous grievances are accepted and investigated. Complainants are protected against any form of retaliation. The college ombudsman, Dr. Meena Patil, handles appeals against SGRC decisions and cases involving serious misconduct.

For academic grievances including exam result disputes, students may apply for revaluation within 10 days of result declaration. The fee of Rs 500 per subject is refundable if the grade changes. Three independent faculty members review revaluation requests.

Contact information for different departments includes info@xyzcollege.edu.in for general queries, exam@xyzcollege.edu.in for examination issues, and the admission helpline at +91 9876 543210. The hostel warden for boys can be reached at +91 9876 543211 and for girls at +91 9876 543212.

Appeals against SGRC decisions may be made to the Principal within 10 days. The Principal decision is final and binding. The college maintains complete records of all grievances and resolutions, reviewed annually by an external audit committee.


Code of Conduct and Discipline

All students are expected to conduct themselves with dignity, integrity, and respect for fellow students, faculty, and staff. The code of conduct applies to all campus activities, college-sponsored events, and online interactions related to the institution.

Students must wear their college identity card visibly at all times on campus. The ID must be shown when requested by any college official or security personnel. Lost identity cards must be reported immediately and a replacement obtained for Rs 500.

Academic integrity is paramount. Plagiarism, cheating, data fabrication, and unauthorized collaboration are serious offences. All assignments are checked using Turnitin software. First offence earns zero marks, second offence results in an F grade, and third offence leads to one semester suspension.

Discrimination or harassment based on gender, religion, caste, ethnicity, disability, or sexual orientation is strictly prohibited. The Internal Complaints Committee constituted under the POSH Act handles harassment complaints with guaranteed confidentiality.

Tobacco, alcohol, and intoxicating substances are banned on campus. First violation incurs a Rs 5,000 fine, and repeat offences lead to one semester suspension. The health centre provides counselling for students with substance use concerns.

Students must use college IT resources responsibly. Downloading copyrighted content, accessing inappropriate material, and attempting network breaches are punishable. Social media posts defaming the institution or individuals attract disciplinary action.

Campus property must be treated with care. Damage to college property will be charged to the responsible student at replacement cost. Graffiti, littering, and vandalism are taken seriously and may result in disciplinary proceedings and fines.


Academic Calendar and Timetable

The academic year at XYZ College is divided into two semesters. The odd semester runs from August to December and the even semester from January to May. Orientation for new students is held in the last week of July before classes begin.

Regular classes run from 9 AM to 4:30 PM, Monday through Friday. Each lecture period is 50 minutes followed by a 10-minute break between sessions. The lunch break is from 12:30 PM to 1:30 PM daily.

Laboratory sessions are scheduled in 2-hour blocks, typically from 2 PM to 4 PM on weekdays. Some departments also hold Saturday lab sessions from 9 AM to 1 PM on alternate weeks. Each student attends 4 to 6 lab sessions per week depending on branch and semester.

Wednesday afternoons from 2 PM to 4:30 PM are reserved for extracurricular activities including club meetings, cultural events, and sports practice. Clubs available include the Coding Club, Robotics Club, Drama Club, Music Club, Sports Club, and Entrepreneurship Cell.

The college observes all national holidays, major festivals including Diwali, Holi, Eid, and Christmas, and provides a one-week mid-semester break. Total holidays per semester are approximately 15 to 20 days. The detailed academic calendar is published on the website by June each year.

Class timetables are available on the student portal under the Academic section and can also be downloaded as PDF from the department notice board or the college mobile app. Timetables are finalised before each semester starts.

Elective subject changes are allowed during the first week of the semester through the academic section. An elective change form signed by both current and new course instructors must be submitted. Changes after the first week require Dean of Academics approval.

The academic calendar also includes dates for mid-term examinations in weeks 6 and 12, assignment submission deadlines, project review schedules, and the end-semester examination period. Students are advised to keep a copy of the calendar for reference throughout the semester.


Anti-Ragging Policy

XYZ College has a strict zero-tolerance policy towards ragging of any kind. Ragging is defined as any act that causes physical, psychological, or emotional harm to a student, including verbal abuse, bullying, intimidation, and forcing students to perform uncomfortable or humiliating acts.

All students must submit a signed anti-ragging affidavit through the antiragging.in portal at the time of admission. Both the student and their parent or guardian must sign this affidavit. Failure to submit the affidavit will result in blocking of admission.

The Anti-Ragging Committee is headed by the Dean of Students and includes senior faculty, hostel wardens, administrative staff, and student representatives. The committee conducts regular inspections of hostels, canteen, and common areas to ensure a ragging-free environment.

Students found guilty of ragging face severe consequences including immediate expulsion from the hostel, suspension from classes for one to four semesters depending on severity, and filing of a First Information Report with the local police under applicable anti-ragging laws.

The national anti-ragging helpline 1800 180 5522 is toll-free and available 24 hours. Students can also report incidents to antiragging@xyzcollege.edu.in or the campus helpline at +91 9876 543225. All complaints are treated with strict confidentiality and the identity of complainants is protected.

Senior students are assigned as mentors to freshers under the buddy system to help new students adjust to college life. Regular awareness programmes and workshops on anti-ragging are conducted during orientation week and throughout the first semester.

Any student who witnesses ragging and fails to report it may also face disciplinary action as per UGC regulations. The college encourages a culture of mutual respect and support among all members of the student community.
